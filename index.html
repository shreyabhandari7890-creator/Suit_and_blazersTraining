Session 01 — Product Knowledge

Mastering the
Art of Suit Wear

Everything a Suit Expert needs to know — from anatomy and construction to sizing and fit. This is your foundation.

Session Goal

TYPES OF SUIT

Blazer
Casual & Formal
Stand-alone jacket, no matching trouser. Worn casually with chinos or formally with dress trousers. Most versatile piece in S&B.
2-Piece Suit
Formal & Wedding/Ceremonial
Jacket + matching trouser. Works for office, interviews, and formal occasions — as well as weddings and ceremonial events.
3-Piece Suit
Mostly Ceremonial & Wedding
Jacket + trouser + waistcoat. Primarily worn for weddings and ceremonial occasions. An elevated, statement look.
Bandhgala
Indian Formal
Stand collar, mandarin style. Ideal for Indian weddings and evening events.
Double Breasted
Formal – Power
Extremely formal. Best on slim frames. Peak lapel standard.

TYPES OF LAPEL

🔷
Notch Lapel
A triangular V-shaped indentation (the "notch") where the collar meets the lapel, creating a step shape. The most common lapel — classic and versatile. Works for both casual and professional looks. Found on most single-breasted PF suits.
▲
Peak Lapel
The lapel extends outward and upward toward the shoulders on both sides, forming a sharp point. Naturally wider and broader than the notch lapel — adds visual width to shoulders and a commanding, formal look. Standard on double-breasted suits. Ideal for weddings and formal occasions.
〇
Shawl Lapel
A smooth, continuous curved edge with no gorge line or notch at all — the collar and lapel are one single unbroken piece. The most elegant and minimalist of the three. Found exclusively on tuxedos and dinner jackets. Used for black-tie and very formal evening events.

JACKET CONSTRUCTION — WHAT GOES INSIDE

Canvas Construction +
  • Chest canvas gives shape, structure and strength
  • Full canvas is the traditional and highest grade of construction
  • Conforms to body shape after multiple wears
  • More durable, breathes better, allows natural drape
  • Layers: Camel Hair → Horse Hair → Horse Tail
  • This is why premium suits justify their price
Lining +
  • Shiny fabric inside the Coat/Blazer
  • Provides ease of movement and prevents creasing
  • Adds structure and weight to the suit
  • Made of Polyester/Acetate/Viscose/Bemberg Twill
  • Sleeve lining = striped; brands use signature lining
Key Trims +
  • Sleeve Head Roll: ensures zero puckering at shoulders
  • Shoulder Pad: shapes contour of shoulder perfectly
  • Under Collar Felt: prevents neck bouncing; easy fit around neck
Why Wool Blends Are Premium +
  • Natural fabric — breathable in summer, warm in winter
  • Great colour retention
  • Very durable — lasts significantly longer
  • Naturally wrinkle-resistant
Vents in Jacket +
  • Single Vent (American): Center vent. Most common. Practical.
  • Double Vents (English): Side vents. Most formal. Best movement.
  • No Vent (Italian): Cleanest silhouette. Less movement.
  • Vents stop the jacket from creasing when moving
Button Rules +
  • 1-button: Always button when standing. Unbutton sitting.
  • 2-button: Top button ALWAYS. Bottom button NEVER.
  • 3-button: Middle ALWAYS. Top optional. Bottom NEVER.
  • Rule of thumb: Sometimes, Always, Never
Tuxedo vs Suit +
  • Tuxedos → bow ties; Suits → with or without a tie
  • Tuxedo lapels are satin; suit lapels match fabric
  • Tuxedos have satin buttons; suits have regular buttons
  • Tuxedo trousers have a satin stripe; suit trousers don't

FIT — THE MOST IMPORTANT THING

Why Right Fit Matters

Comfort · Makes you stand out · Hides asymmetry and flaws · Highlights shoulders and chest · Gives a natural V shape · Builds confidence. A well-fitted suit is always the best suit regardless of price.

6-inch Drop Rule

Blazer to trouser = 6 inches. Blazer size 38 → Trouser 32. Blazer 42 → Trouser 36. Always verify this when selling a suit.

FIT CHECK POINTS

  • Jacket sleeve: 1 inch shirt cuff visible
  • Trouser: one crease at shoe = perfect length
  • Shoulder seam sits at edge of shoulder — not hanging
  • Jacket collar smooth against shirt collar
  • Trouser waist: 1 finger insert under waistband
  • No pulling at chest when buttoned

HOW TO TAKE MEASUREMENTS

📏
Chest Measurement
Wrap the measuring tape around the widest part of the chest.

Run the tape under the arms — not over them.

Insert 1–2 fingers under the tape before finalising the suit size to allow ease of movement.
↔️
Shoulder Measurement
Run the tape across the back from one tip of the shoulder to the other.

The tape should follow the natural curve of the shoulders.

Take a helping hand if necessary for accuracy.
💪
Sleeve Measurement
Keep the arm relaxed at the side.

Measure from the shoulder seam down the outside of the arm.

Stop at the base of the thumb — that is the ideal sleeve end point.

Session 03 — Hosting + Selling New

The Suit Expert
Experience

A wedding customer doesn't just buy a suit — they buy an experience. How you host them is as important as what you show them.

The Reality of a Wedding Customer

Wedding customers spend 60+ minutes in the S&B section. They bring family. A long visit without conversion is a staffing issue AND a lost sale. The way you host — water, seating, warmth — determines whether they buy or walk.

THE HOSTING PROTOCOL

💧
Greet + Water Within 5 Min
As soon as a customer enters the S&B section, greet them warmly and offer water on a tray within 5 minutes. This is non-negotiable. It sets the tone for the entire visit.
🛋️
Seat the Family First
The decision to buy a wedding suit is always a family decision. Offer seating before you show any product. A comfortable family is a buying family.
🎯
Ask the Occasion
Before showing any garment: "Which function is this for, Sir?" The wedding ritual (water, seating, full consultation) is only triggered after the CCA confirms it is a wedding visit.
🎁
Gift at Purchase
On purchase, present a chocolate box (₹150–200) as a groom's token of celebration. This small gesture creates a memorable moment and drives word-of-mouth. Funded by the Wedding Zone activation.

LOS MANAGEMENT — CRITICAL New

What is LOS?

Loss of Sale (LOS) — a customer who visits the S&B section but does not make a purchase. Every LOS must be logged. Capturing this data drives follow-up and reveals conversion gaps.

WALK-OUT PROTOCOL

  • Every walk-out must be logged in the LOS register by name
  • Note reason if possible: price, size unavailable, came back later
  • Log must happen same day — no next-day entry
  • SM reviews LOS register at end of each day
  • Target: 100% same-day logging

48-HR FOLLOW-UP

  • WhatsApp follow-up within 48 hours of walk-out
  • Personalise: use their name, reference the occasion
  • Offer to hold a style or check size availability
  • Do not push — invite back for a better consultation
  • SM tracks follow-up completion weekly

SUIT BASICS — THE 11 RULES

  • Shirt cuffs should NOT cover your hands
  • Unfasten your jacket when you sit down
  • NEVER fasten the last button on your suit jacket
  • Jacket collar should fit smoothly against shirt collar
  • Shirt sleeves should slightly peek beneath the jacket (1 inch)
  • If wearing a tie, button the shirt all the way to the top
  • Widest part of tie = widest part of lapel
  • Tip of tie should touch the top of your waistband
  • Trousers should NEVER look baggy
  • Shoes matched according to the colour of your belt
  • Pocket square colour should contrast (not match) your suit

PRODUCT CARE — WHAT TO TELL CUSTOMERS

👔
Storing a Suit
Always hang on a wide wooden hanger — never wire. Keep in a suit bag. Never fold. Allow suit to breathe overnight between wears.
♨️
Ironing & Steaming
Steam is preferred over ironing for suits — prevents shining. If ironing, always use a pressing cloth. Never iron directly on the fabric.
🧹
Cleaning
Dry clean only for suits — not machine wash. Spot clean small marks with a damp cloth. Dry clean at end of season, not after every wear.

TIE & POCKET SQUARE — QUICK REFERENCE

Tie Length Rule

The tip of the tie should touch the top of the waistband — not above it, not below it. This is one of the most common errors customers make. Correct it during the trial.

Pocket Square Folds

Three folds to know: Square Fold (classic formal) · Triangle/Point Fold (smart formal) · Puff Fold (occasion/wedding). Keep a practice pocket square at the counter.
